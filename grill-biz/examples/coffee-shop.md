# Lean Canvas: Coffee Shop (Roast & Code)

## 1. Problem
* Developers lack a quiet workspace with premium specialty coffee.
* Generic coffee shops are noisy and have poor Wi-Fi.
* Existing co-working spaces lack a high-quality coffee culture.
* **Existing Alternatives:** Starbucks, local library, standard co-working spaces.

## 2. Customer Segments
* Software developers and programmers.
* Solo founders and remote tech workers.
* Freelance UI/UX designers.
* **Early Adopters:** Remote devs who spend >$20/day on coffee and value quiet, fast internet.

## 3. Unique Value Proposition
* A premium specialty espresso bar built specifically inside a high-speed, silent co-working lounge.
* **High-Level Concept:** Starbucks meets WeWork, but quieter and with better beans.

## 4. Solution
* High-speed fiber-optic Wi-Fi (symmetric 1Gbps).
* Soundproof call booths and silent work zones.
* Specialty single-origin espresso and pour-over coffee included in the lounge fee.

## 5. Channels
* Local tech meetups and developer associations.
* Targeted social media ads (LinkedIn/Twitter) targeting remote workers in the zip code.
* Word of mouth/referral discounts for tech teams.

## 6. Revenue Streams
* Monthly membership subscription ($150/month).
* Day passes ($25/day).
* Retail sales of roasted coffee beans.

## 7. Cost Structure
* Commercial space lease (fixed cost).
* Barista labor and premium coffee bean sourcing.
* Internet bandwidth and soundproofing hardware.

## 8. Key Metrics
* Monthly recurring revenue (MRR).
* Customer churn rate.
* Average daily occupancy rate.

## 9. Unfair Advantage
* Exclusive partnership with local award-winning roasters.
* Proprietary booking app allowing members to reserve quiet seats and soundproof call booths in advance.
