# Lean Canvas: EcoThreads

## 1. Problem
* Fast fashion causes massive environmental waste and pollution.
* Sustainable clothing is often overpriced and unaffordable.
* Lack of modern, trendy design styles in eco-friendly apparel.
* **Existing Alternatives:** Traditional fast fashion brands (Zara, H&M), premium sustainable brands (Patagonia - high price).

## 2. Customer Segments
* Environmentally conscious Gen-Z and Millennial consumers.
* Eco-activists and green lifestyle enthusiasts.
* **Early Adopters:** Young professionals (ages 22-35) who buy clothes online regularly and actively seek eco-friendly labels.

## 3. Unique Value Proposition
* Ultra-soft, highly durable bamboo-fiber activewear and apparel that matches fast-fashion prices without the environmental cost.
* **High-Level Concept:** Patagonia comfort at H&M prices.

## 4. Solution
* E-commerce storefront with transparent carbon-footprint tracking.
* Sustainable bamboo-fiber fabrics (organic, hypoallergenic).
* Closed-loop circular recycling program (return old clothes for store credit).

## 5. Channels
* Organic Instagram/TikTok micro-influencer partnerships.
* Paid Meta & Pinterest social ads targeting sustainable keywords.
* Email newsletters and lifestyle blogs.

## 6. Revenue Streams
* Direct-to-consumer (D2C) e-commerce product sales.
* Circular recycle trade-ins (refurbished line).

## 7. Cost Structure
* Raw materials (organic bamboo fibers, non-toxic dyes).
* Manufacturing partners (certified ethical factories).
* Shipping, eco-friendly packaging, and Shopify store fees.

## 8. Key Metrics
* Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) and Lifetime Value (LTV).
* Shopping cart conversion rate.
* Repeat customer rate.

## 9. Unfair Advantage
* Proprietary sourcing agreement with bamboo forests in Asia, cutting fabric wholesale costs by 45%.
